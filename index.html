A Look at the Human Impact of Parklets
and the People Who Make Them

What’s a parklet?

Tiny green spaces—only 10 feet by 20 feet—pepper some San Francisco neighborhoods. These mini-parks are parklets, they replace one to three parking spots with plant-filled seating areas that are open to the public. Each one is sponsored by a member of the public who works with their neighbors and the city to fund, plan, build, and maintain the small structure and the plants within.
